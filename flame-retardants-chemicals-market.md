<p><p>The flame retardants chemicals market is categorized into non-halogenated and halogenated flame retardants. Non-halogenated flame retardants, often derived from phosphorous or nitrogen compounds, are preferred for their environmental safety and reduced toxicity. They are commonly used in electronics and textiles. In contrast, halogenated flame retardants, containing chlorine or bromine, are effective in reducing flammability but raise environmental and health concerns due to potential toxicity. Each type serves diverse applications across industries like construction, automotive, and electronics.</p></p>
