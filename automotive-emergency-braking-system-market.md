<p><p>The automotive emergency braking system market comprises two primary types: foot brakes and hand brakes. Foot brakes are the traditional system used in most vehicles, activated by the driver’s foot, allowing for quick and effective halting in critical situations. Hand brakes, or parking brakes, are typically engaged manually and serve as an additional safety measure to immobilize the vehicle when parked or during emergencies. Both systems are essential for enhancing vehicle safety and preventing accidents, particularly in unexpected braking scenarios.</p></p>
